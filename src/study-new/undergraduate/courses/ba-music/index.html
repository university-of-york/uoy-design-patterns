Our BA in Music delivers a balanced musical education taught by active professionals at the forefront of current research. There are many opportunities to practice your talents, from the first year Practical Project, to ensemble performances, to our major concert series.

General

You'll undertake a mixture of musicology and analysis, composition and performance, with the opportunity to specialise. Our modular project system allows you to tailor your degree by choosing areas of study that suit your interests.

All students take solo and practical studies throughout their degree, which include solo and ensemble performances, critical listening and reflective work about your instrumental/vocal progress. 

In addition, students attend weekly instrumental/vocal workshops, composers' and research seminars. 

Year 1

In the first year, you'll develop a core body of skills in two skill-based modules and one module of your choice, plus you'll participate in the first-year Practical Project. 

  • First-year modules
  • Practical Project

Academic integrity module

In addition to the above you will also need to complete our online Academic Integrity module. This covers some of the essential skills and knowledge which will help you to study independently and produce work of a high academic standard which is vital for success at York.

This module will:

  • define academic integrity and academic misconduct
  • explain why and when you should reference source material and other people's work
  • provide interactive exercises to help you to assess whether you've understood the concepts
  • provide answers to FAQs and links to useful resources.

Year 2

Each term you'll select from a wide range of modules to study topics in more depth. In the first half of the term, you’ll attend intensive taught sessions under the guidance of course tutors, who are all active researchers in their specialist fields. During the second half you’ll work with tutorial help to produce a portfolio of work.

Undergraduate modules

Year 3

In addition to undergraduate modules, you'll also undertake a substantial piece of work that you devise and research on your own, guided by a project advisor. This might take the form of an extended essay, a piece of analytical work, a theatre production, a community project, a transcription or editing, or an album.

Teaching and assessmentHow you’ll be taught and assessed

Teaching format

We score highly in the National Student Survey for our teaching approach, which focuses on small groups and developing you as an independent critical learner. You'll learn through a range of lectures, seminars, performances, workshops, classes, discussions and group study. 

  • The first year Practical Project involves around 50 hours of rehearsals, lectures, workshops, tutorials and an assessed performance.
  • You will spend about three hours per week in ensemble performance and critical listening work.
  • Individual instrumental/vocal tuition is set at 15 hours per year in years one and two, and 18 hours for year three.

Assessments

The work you submit for assessment varies, but usually includes some of the following:

  • Essay of approximately 5,000 words
  • Transcript of seminars/presentations given during the project
  • Coursework
  • Original compositions and/or style study examples
  • Transcription, editing and realisation
  • Practical performance.

The final degree classification is calculated based on your five modules, solo project, and a recital or composition folio. You can opt to take another module or a second independent study module rather than giving a recital or submitting a composition folio.

Careers Where you’ll go from here

The majority of our BA in Music students go on to graduate-level jobs in music and related professions. Students also opt to undertake further study for an MA, PhD, conservatoire programme or PGCE.

Some of our recent graduates have taken careers as:

  • BBC Singers Assistant
  • Freelance Jazz musician with the World Service Project 
  • Opera Direction for Opera North, Glyndebourne Touring Opera and the Royal Opera House
  • BBC Production (Proms and Radio 3 & 4)
  • Music publishing 
  • Major music agencies (including Hazard Chase)
  • UK Concert Director of the Philharmonia Orchestra
  • London Symphony Orchestra
  • Music Outreach Officer at Cambridge University
  • Lecturers in universities in the UK and US.
