Explore our research themes

Our reputation for research excellence – we are ranked in the top ten institutions for research that has a positive impact – is founded on our passion to make a difference. 

The seven pillars of our research strategy align our academic strengths to best meet the grand scientific, social and environmental challenges of our time.

Related links

  • Research strategy 2015‑2020

Creativity

Creativity is a key driver of modern, dynamic societies. Our research ranges from the molecular and cellular workings of the brain, through to the linguistic, cultural, aesthetic and cognitive dimensions of what it is to be creative.

Dr Damian Murphy is the Research Champion for Creativity.

Explore Creativity

Culture and communication

From prehistory to the digital age, our researchers are uncovering the deep roots of culture, communication, social identity, shedding light on the past, and forging predictive models of the future.

Dr Mark Jenner is the Research Champion for Culture and communication.

Explore Culture and communication

Environmental sustainability and resilience

Whether it is food scarcity and energy security, or climate change and pollution, our research is providing the evidence base for policy makers to shape a more sustainable approach to economic growth and development.

Professor Sue Hartley is the Research Champion for Environmental sustainability and resilience.

Explore Environmental sustainability and resilience

Health and wellbeing

Our global reputation in biomedicine, health science and economics is built on research excellence that begins with the fundamental understanding of health and disease, and progresses to evidence-based decision-making about healthcare policies and therapies.

Professor Karen Bloor is the Research Champion for Health and wellbeing.

Explore Health and wellbeing

Justice and equality

Fairness, inclusivity, equality, and welfare, are policy fields where our research sets the political agenda. These themes also define our values and approach to meeting the grand social challenges of our time at national and international level.

Professor Kate Pickett is the Research Champion for Justice and equality.

Explore Justice and equality

Risk, evidence and decision making

In an increasingly risk-averse world, our research is penetrating deep into how decisions on risk are made, how they are managed and monitored, and how technology and social science can reduce risk – from traffic accidents to terrorist incidents.

Professor John McDermid is the Research Champion for Risk, evidence and decision making.

Explore Risk, evidence and decision making

Technologies for the future

Our researchers are developing novel technologies, processes and materials with the potential to transform the economic, environmental, and social landscape. From precision plasma manufacturing, to plants that can devour toxins, fundamental research is being rapidly translated into real world solutions.

Professor Thomas Krauss is the Research Champion for Technologies for the future.

Explore Technologies for the future
